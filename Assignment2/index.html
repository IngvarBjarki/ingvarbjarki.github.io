Assignment 2A: One scatter plot and two datasets

Description

The graph above shows the number of prostitution instances in San Francisco, plotted against the number of vehicle theft instances in the same city. The points on the graph each represent a district within the city. The size of the points are proportional to the number of crimes within the district. Click the green button to toggle between the years 2003 and 2015 to see how the crime frequency changes between these years.

Explain in your own words why you think I want the axes to be the same for both years?

If the axes would change depending on the maximum x- and y-values for each of the years, the distance that the points travel when you toggle between years would not be representative of the change in crime frequency. The visualization could then be misleading in terms of represting the change in crime frequency between the two years.
